  • Rules of the Game
    • Set Up

      Each player is dealt 7 cards at the start of the game. To initialize players, either press the new game button or press "Play Uno!" in the navigation.

    • Game Play

      There are four color suits, red, yellow, green, and blue. For each color suit, there are 10 number cards, 0-9, in addition to reverse, skip, and draw two cards.
      During each player's turn, they must play a card, by clicking on it, that matches either the color suit or the value, numeric or action, of the card on the top of the discard pile. If they do not have a card that matches, or a wild card, they must draw a card by clicking the Draw Card button.

    • To win, Be the first player to have an empty hand!
  • Card-Specific Functionality
    • Wild Cards

      Wild cards allow the player to choose a color suit and can be played on any card. Wild card draw fours also force the next player to draw four cards.

    • Skip Card

      When played, skip cards skip the next player's turn.

    • Draw Card

      When played, draw cards force the next player to draw two cards.

    • Reverse Card

      When played, reverse cards revese the order of play, returning to the previous player.

UNO!

Set-Up:

On the next screen, you'll select a number of players and enter player names.
Once the game begins, each player will be dealt seven cards and a final card will be added to the discard pile.
The object of the game is to be the first player to have an empty hand.

Game Play:

There are four color suits, red, yellow, green, and blue. For each color suit, there are 10 number cards, 0-9, in addition to two reverse cards, skip cards, and draw two cards.
During each player's turn, they must play a card, by clicking on it, that matches either the color suit or the value, numeric or action, of the card on the top of the discard pile. If they do not have a card that matches, or a wild card, they must draw a card by clicking the Draw Card button.

Wild Card

Wild Cards, and Wild Card Draw Fours, allow the player to choose what color suit they would like to change the discard card to. These cards are usually used when the player does not have a card of the current color suit. Wild Card Draw Fours have the additional functionality of forcing the next player to draw four cards.

Action Cards

Reverse Cards: when this card is played, the order of play will reverse, making it the previous player's turn. This new order will continue until another reverse card is played.
Skip Cards: when this card is played, the game will skip over the next player's turn.
Draw Two Cards: when this card is played, it will force the next player to draw two cards.
